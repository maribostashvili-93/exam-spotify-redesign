As they evolved from '90s French house pioneers to 2000s dance tastemakers to mainstream heroes in the 2010s, Daft Punk remained one of dance music's most iconic acts. With their early singles and 1997's influential debut album Homework, Guy-Manuel de Homem-Christo and Thomas Bangalter quickly won acclaim for their skill of blending new-school house, pop, funk, and hip-hop into nostalgic yet futuristic forms.

Not content to just widen electronic music's popularity, 2001's Discovery reinvented the form and set new international standards of spectacle, while 2013's Random Access Memories created a polished pop future with a deeply human center.
